Developing the Per and Polyfluoroalkyl Substance IMplementor for Mass Spectrometry (PIMMS)

a novel feature processing and visualization platform for PFAS analysis

Head Developer

September 2024 - Present

Per and Polyfluoroalkyl Substances (PFAS) are toxic contaminants of concern. Currently, efforts to detect new PFAS products in clinical sampling relies on manual data analysis with poor discovery rates. PIMMS was developed to accelerate this workflow, prioritizing and vizualizing features of interest. PIMMS is a novel software and has already been used to identify emerging PFAS in animals and humans. Already, PIMMS has been able to reduce a workflow that takes months to days enabling a more indepth investigation into the exposure we face from PFAS.

Targeted and Non-Targeted Analyses of PFAS in Human Serum Samples Collected Yearly from 2003 to 2021

Detecting links between environmental contaminants and autoimmune disease

Head Researcher

January 2024 - Present

Recent evidence has linked several PFAS to adverse health outcomes, a potential link is feared between PFAS exposure and the onset of autoimmune disease. Previous epidemiological studies have only been able to rely exclusively on "target" analysis, focusing on a handful of already extensively characterized PFAS. This project is an entirely new approach to epidemiological PFAS studies using a "non-targeted" all features approach to identify new PFAS of concern in individuals and establish links to 5 of the most concerning autoimmune diseases in the US.
